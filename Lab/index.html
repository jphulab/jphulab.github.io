Team Profile:

At present, the Biological Intelligence and Control Research Center has 2 professors, 1 associate professor, 1 lecturer, 11 doctoral students, 14 master students, including 1 national talent. The team is mainly engaged in multi-intelligent system modeling and control, unmanned autonomous control, robotics and machine vision research. In recent years, the team has published more than 100 high-level papers, including more than 60 SCI papers; 6 authorized invention patents and 9 patented inventions; It has undertaken a number of scientific research projects such as the National Key Research and Development Plan, the National QR Plan, the National Natural Science Foundation, and the provincial and ministerial scientific research Plan. Team members have served as IEEE Fellow and special committee members in important academic organizations such as IEEE and China Association of Automation, and editorial board members in high-level journals such as Journal of Systems Science and Complexity and Kybernetika. Team members have also maintained close cooperation with renowned scholars from international universities such as Texas Tech University in the United States, Royal Institute of Technology in Sweden, City University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, University of Western Sydney in Australia and Sophia University in Japan.

Research Characteristics:

Based on the theory of multi-agent system, cluster control and intelligent control are studied and applied in robot system. With biological systems as the research object, artificial intelligence as the support and automation technology as the core, the research center is engaged in the basic and application research of new theories, new methods and new technologies in the fields of brain intelligence control, machine vision and multi-sensor information fusion, multi-agent system coordinated control, and robot intelligent control.
